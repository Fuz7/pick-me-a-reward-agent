Definition:
Leisure activities are pleasure-based behaviors used after completing academic tasks or personal goals, ranked according to perceived guilt and stimulation. Rewards must always be leisure-focused, never productive or skill-improving.

Guilt Rankings:

Low-guilt rewards: relaxing and minimally disruptive
Examples: listening to music, taking short walks, stretching, lying down to rest, watching short videos, looking at memes, drinking coffee or tea, sitting outside, casual chatting, taking a nap or daydreaming.

Medium-guilt rewards: moderate indulgence
Examples: casual or single-player gaming, watching a full episode of a series, watching a movie, scrolling social media for limited time, browsing YouTube, listening to podcasts, eating sweets or fast food, ordering milk tea or coffee, window-shopping online, reading comics or manga, drawing for fun, journaling casually, light role-playing or simulation games.

High-guilt rewards: highly stimulating or time-consuming
Examples: long gaming sessions, competitive or online multiplayer gaming, ranked matches, binge-watching multiple episodes, doomscrolling social media, watching streams for hours, excessive snacking, late-night gaming, staying up very late for entertainment, repeatedly refreshing feeds.

Task Matching:

Low-guilt → small or routine tasks

Medium-guilt → moderately difficult goals

High-guilt → significant milestones

Novelty Component:

Add 3 extra surprise rewards not in the standard examples for variety and delight.
Example: attending a random online live trivia event, trying a themed AR/VR mini-game, exploring a short mystery story app.

Additional Potential Preferences (User-Specific Random Ideas):

Visiting a quirky café or dessert place

Participating in a mini scavenger hunt online or offline

Listening to a live virtual concert or street performance

Exploring unusual playlists or podcasts

Testing new casual mobile apps

Quick craft or DIY fun activities (origami, slime, miniature building kits)

Trying a new dessert recipe at home

Engaging in light interactive fiction or text-based games.

Add things that are also not on this list.

Reward Guidelines:

Always proportional to effort

Always pleasure-based, not productive

Include novelty rewards occasionally

Ensure emotional alignment with user’s experience (guilt/stimulation match)
